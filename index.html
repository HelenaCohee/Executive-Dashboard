What's Working
Culture orientation landed. Both days, both audiences.
Ownership, Bold Bets, Decision Making — attendees left with a felt sense of company values, not just a slide deck version. This is the program's core job. It's working.
⟳
Design Signal
Passive absorption isn't learning. Attendees already know this.
Both days drew consistent requests for table exercises, role-plays, and peer dialogue. Adults don't change behavior by watching slides.
⏱
Systemic Gap
Arriving 3–5 months in means the hardest moments are already behind them.
The program is designed as orientation — but most attendees are already oriented. They've navigated their first feedback conversation without the framing this program would have given them.
→
Unmet Need
The program ends. The need for community and continuity doesn't.
Resource summaries, Slack cohorts, 30-day follow-ups — requested unprompted by both audiences. They're asking for something that says: this conversation doesn't have to end.

Gaps & Priorities

Click any item to expand. Gaps are design questions worth getting curious about. Priorities are split by who owns the answer.

⬡ 3 Gaps From Q1
Missing StoryWe only told the wins. Attendees noticed.
⌄

New employees are smart. When a program only shows success stories, they don't feel inspired — they feel managed. Both days would benefit from one real pivot: what changed, why it failed first, and what was learned.

Both Days · Content Design
Content DesignNon-technical employees attended a program not fully built for them.
⌄

Legal, Finance, and Communications make up ~20% of both rooms. One bridge moment — a facilitated question or a single slide — changes the felt experience for a fifth of the room.

Day 1 · ~20% of attendees affected
Strategic Question0 of 5 VP+ attended. Before we escalate, we should get curious.
⌄

Is this the right format for VP+ onboarding? A separate executive track — different structure, different cadence — may be more effective.

Both Days · Requires leadership conversation
✓ We Own — Q2 Delivery Changes
SurveyShorten the survey. Add a QR code. Capture feedback in the room.
⌄

Day 2's 40% response rate was driven by in-room distribution. Day 1 had 33% abandonment. Fix: 5 questions max, QR on the final slide, 3 minutes before dismissal.

Owner: Program Lead · Before Q2
EmailCut the post-event email to 5 links max. One featured CTA.
⌄

Day 2's email had 17 links. 13 got zero clicks. For Q2: survey as the hero CTA, two curated resources, done.

Owner: Program Lead · Ready pre-Q2
OwnershipName one owner per day. Not a team. One person.
⌄

Day 1 and Day 2 are distinct programs. Shared ownership is where accountability quietly disappears.

Owner: Program Team · March 2026
↗ Needs Leadership Conversation
CapacityTwo cohorts or alternative model? Decision needed before Q2 locks.
⌄

At the likely scenario, Q2 demand hits 505 — approaching the venue's single-day limit.

Raise to: Global Program Lead
VP+ PathwayShould VP+ have a separate executive onboarding journey?
⌄

0 of 5 VP+ attended. A separate executive track may be more effective and respectful.

Raise to: Leadership Team
ContinuityAdd a continuity layer — Slack cohort, 30-day follow-up, or resource digest.
⌄

Both audiences asked unprompted for something after the event. Low-lift, high-signal investments that extend the program beyond the room.

Raise to: Global Program Team
The Bottom Line
The program proved it can deliver the moment.
Now it needs to deliver what comes after it.
What Q1 Proved465 attended Day 1, 75 attended Day 2. Near-perfect show-up. 4.31 and 4.49 satisfaction. 61 volunteered for focus groups. The foundation is solid.
What the Data SaysThe event works. What comes after doesn't exist yet. No structured follow-up. No senior leadership signal in the room. The program ends when the real learning should begin.
What's NextFix what we control: survey, day owners, content bridge. Raise what we can't: capacity, VP+ pathway, continuity design. The goal: evolve from a calendar moment into a sustained journey.
